Administrator guide

Routine Writing Lab management should be completed through the administrator portal. Administrators should not need GitHub or Supabase for ordinary yearly operations.

Keep at least two active administrators whenever possible so the organization is not dependent on one person.

Appointments

  • Review upcoming student appointments and assigned consultants.
  • Retrieve and copy a booking reference when a student loses it.
  • Automatically reassign an appointment when another eligible consultant is available.
  • Cancel an appointment and enter a clear reason when appropriate.

Confirm the student’s name and Houston ISD email address before providing a lost booking reference.

Consultants

  • Create consultant accounts from the administrator portal.
  • Update display names and weekly shifts.
  • Deactivate consultants who are no longer serving.
  • Reactivate returning consultants without rebuilding their record.

Deactivation is generally preferable to deletion because it preserves scheduling history and allows the consultant to return later.

Accounts and roles

  • Accounts may be consultant-only, administrator-only, or have both roles.
  • A person with an existing login can later receive administrator or consultant access.
  • New accounts receive a temporary password that must be changed after the first sign-in.
  • Administrators can reset a forgotten password from the portal.

Give temporary passwords directly to account holders. Never place passwords in public documents or shared student materials.

Regular weekly schedule

The standard schedule currently contains two 30-minute appointment slots on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday:

  • 11:45 AM–12:15 PM
  • 12:15 PM–12:45 PM

Shift times should be changed only when the Writing Lab’s operating schedule changes. A stored shift cannot be deleted while consultants are assigned to it.

Calendar and schedule changes

  • Remove holidays and other closed days from the stored lab calendar.
  • Add a special Thursday or another exception as an individual lab date.
  • Include a student-facing note when special hours need explanation.
  • Close one dated appointment slot when only part of a normal lab period is available.
  • Reopen a dated slot when the exception is no longer needed.

Upcoming exceptions appear automatically on the student scheduler within its 14-day booking window.

Starting a new school year

  1. Create the new school year using its first and last student days.
  2. Generate the regular Monday–Wednesday lab dates.
  3. Remove holidays, testing days, and other dates when the lab will be closed.
  4. Add any known special dates or schedule notes.
  5. Make the new school year active.
  6. Confirm the correct year appears on the student and consultant pages.
  7. Confirm active consultants have the correct weekly shifts.

Leadership handoff

  1. Create the incoming administrator’s website account.
  2. Confirm they can sign in and change their temporary password.
  3. Confirm they can see appointments, consultants, school years, and calendar controls.
  4. Have them practice creating and deactivating a temporary account.
  5. Keep the outgoing administrator active until the incoming administrator confirms full access.
  6. Deactivate the outgoing administrator when the handoff is complete.

Technical emergency access

Routine administrators do not need GitHub or Supabase. However, at least one trusted long-term technical custodian should retain access in case hosting, billing, security, or database repairs are ever required.

Transfer access by inviting the custodian through the services’ official collaborator or organization controls. Do not share one person’s password.

Privacy and safety

  • Use student information only for Writing Lab scheduling and visit preparation.
  • Sign out after using the administrator portal on a shared device.
  • Do not publish appointment lists, student emails, booking references, or passwords.
  • Keep administrator access limited to people who genuinely need it.
